Section I — About

330 million Americans have grievances.
It's time to file them.

In May 2026, the U.S. Department of Justice under Acting Attorney General Todd Blanche announced the creation of the Anti-Weaponization Fund — a $1.776 billion taxpayer-funded compensation program to pay individuals who claim they were improperly targeted by federal employees and agents.

Here's what they're not leading with: this fund was created as a direct concession in the agreement between Donald Trump and the Department of Justice regarding his $10 billion lawsuit against the Internal Revenue Service. Trump filed that lawsuit after a contractor leaked his tax return information to the media. In exchange for withdrawing his suit, the Justice Department agreed to establish a fund overseen by a five-member commission appointed entirely by Todd Blanche — Trump's own Acting Attorney General.

"Democrats and government watchdogs immediately called the arrangement 'corrupt' and unprecedented, warning it opens the door to meritless claims of political persecution and would use public funds to enrich people close to the president."

The fund is drawn from the federal judgment fund. You, the American taxpayer, are paying for it. It operates through December 15, 2028, and is positioned to primarily benefit Trump allies and associates who claim they were politically targeted by federal agencies — the very people who cheered on the dismantling of those agencies.

We believe this fund is corrupt, unjust, and an abuse of public funds. We believe most Americans agree. And we believe the most powerful thing we can do right now is make that opposition impossible to ignore.

Why the humor?

This is not a joke. It is a satirical protest document — and the satire is intentional.

Humor gives people a way to stay engaged when outrage becomes exhausting. It lowers fear, builds connection, spreads quickly, and makes authoritarian absurdity look exactly as ridiculous as it is.

The humor is the delivery system. The grievance is real.

What We're Doing — And Why Paper

We are not sending emails. We are not signing online petitions that disappear into a database. We are mailing paper. Actual envelopes. Actual stamps. Actual signatures delivered to the actual desk of the Acting Attorney General of the United States.

Every envelope that arrives at the Department of Justice is a physical object that must be received, logged, opened, read, and processed. It cannot be bulk-deleted. It cannot be filtered to spam. It has to be handled — by human beings, one at a time.

Our goal is 16.5 million filings — 5% of the American population. At just 5 minutes per application, processed over a standard 5-day work week, a single processor would need 661 years to work through them. A team of 100 dedicated staff would take nearly 7 years. That is the point. We want them to feel every single one of us.

"I respectfully request that this application receive the careful, individual attention it warrants."

Download Form WTAF-8647, check every box that applies to you, and mail it directly to Acting Attorney General Todd Blanche. Then come back here and log your participation so we can track the count in real time.

A note on your envelope: The form is three pages — two if you print double-sided — but your envelope doesn't have to be thin. A standard Forever stamp covers up to 1 oz, so feel free to include extra pages: blank paper, your kid's coloring book pages, a used grocery list, a receipt from your last shopping trip, your most recent gas station receipt, printed news articles that inspired you to file, or a personal note in your own words telling them exactly how you feel. If you're willing to pay a little extra postage, go for it. Your total cost of participation is a little time and the price of a single stamp — and if that's not the most efficient protest you've ever made, we don't know what is. If you're especially committed, send it via registered mail and require a signature with a return receipt card. Make them sign for every single one.

Section II — How to Participate

Four steps. One stamp.
330 million voices.

This is the easiest protest you'll ever do. It costs one stamp and about ten minutes.

01

Download

Download Form WTAF-8647 below. Print it on standard 8.5×11 paper. It prints on three pages — two if you print double-sided.

02

Fill It Out

Check every box that applies to you. Fill in your name and address. Sign it. The form provides space for additional grievances.

03

Mail It

Fold it in thirds, seal it in a standard envelope, affix a Forever stamp, and mail it to the Acting Attorney General at the DOJ. The address is on the form.

04

Register

Come back and register your participation so we can track how many Americans have filed. Add your name to the record.

Extra credit will be awarded for names that clear the pun bar AND still look plausible on a federal form. Bonus points if it takes them a full second to get it.
